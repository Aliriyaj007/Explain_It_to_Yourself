Why This Works

Active recall: Writing forces you to retrieve information from memory, not just recognize it. This strengthens learning far more than re-reading.

Elaboration: When you explain something in your own words, you connect it to what you already know, making it stick.

The Feynman Technique: Named after physicist Richard Feynman, this method exposes gaps in understanding. If you can't explain it simply, you're still learning it.
